Test Parameters

Leukocytes

Leukocytes (white blood cells - WBC) are the cells of the immune system that are involved in protecting the body against both infectious diseases and foreign invaders. A few white blood cells are normally present in urine and generally yield negative results. When the number of WBCs in urine increases significantly, this screening test will become positive. High levels of WBCs in the urine suggest an infection, such as a UTI.

Other information

Step 1

Remove the strip

Tear open the pouch and remove the strip out of the bag. Each strip is individually packed which ensures a reliable test every time.

Step 2

Remove the strip

Immerse the strip into the urine sample and remove immediately by dragging the edge of the strip against the container rim to remove excess urine. Always use fresh urine specimen in a clean, dry container.

Step 2

Start timing

Place the strip horizontally on a paper towel or tissue to remove excess urine and wait until results are ready to be read. First test is ready in 30 seconds. The reading time for different tests is provided on the package insert.

Step 4

Start timing

Compare the color of each test pad to the corresponding row of color chart on the package insert to find the closest match. Read the results carefully within 60 seconds in a good light.
